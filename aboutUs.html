About Central PA Estate & Elder Law

Central PA Estate & Elder Law PC, (CPAEEL) is an estate planning and elder law firm founded by Robert P. Grubb, Esquire in 2011. Although the firm is relatively new, Mr. Grubb brings over 15 years of estate, trust and elder law experience in Central Pennsylvania. Having worked for a number of law firms, and hundreds of clients, attorney Grubb brings the best of those experiences to the development of Central PA Estate & Elder Law and the needs of the clients we serve.


Central PA Estate & Elder Law’s main office is in the heart of Lancaster County, in the Greenfield Corporate Center. Set in a professional atmosphere with handicapped access and easy parking, the office provides a comfortable and convenient setting to meet with and address client needs. A satellite office is located in Mechanicsburg, Cumberland County, a few miles south of Camp Hill just off of Route 15 providing easy access from the entire Harrisburg region.

Attorney Profile

Robert P. Grubb, is a veteran elder law, trust and estate attorney with more than 15 years of experience serving hundreds of clients and families throughout the Central Pennsylvania region. A native of Philadelphia, Bob settled in the mid-state after college, where he met his wife, Gail, and together they raise their four children. A graduate of Philadelphia Biblical University, Bob entered into Christian ministry upon graduation. Eventfully, Bob attended and graduated from the Widener University School of Law, Harrisburg, PA.

From the beginning of his legal career Bob has always focused on estate and trust planning, estate administration and elder law. Attorney Grubb has authored and contributed to multiple articles and publications, including "The Wills and Trusts Workbook" and "Trust and Asset Protection Alternatives in Pennsylvania". A teacher at heart, Bob excells at putting difficult legal concepts into terms those who are not legally trained can understand. Bob enjoys a significant and dedicated clientele. Attorney Grubb is admitted to practice before all of the Courts of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania and the United States District Court for the Middle District of Pennsylvania. Attorney Grubb belongs to a number of professional associations including the National Academy of Elder Law Attorneys, the Pennsylvania Association of Elder Law Attorneys and Elder Law Answers.

Client-Centered Practice Ideal

CPAEEL's business model is being built upon the principal of a client-centered practice, which is designed more to address the needs of our clients, then the needs of the firm. Our primary focus is you and not us. We have developed the following eight practice ideals which exemplify and define our client-centered practice model. We shall endeavor to implement best practices to achieve these ideals as we work for our clients on their matter.

Sensitive, Respectful, Rapport

We will treat you with respect and understanding. As a senior, you have earned and deserve nothing less. We value you and will demonstrate that value in the work we do for you. We will talk with you in terms and language you can understand. Sometimes, the issues and challenges seniors face are difficult and even embarrassing. Family is not always helpful and sometimes hurtful. We understand this and will treat you with dignity, respect and understanding, even when you feel otherwise. We will work for you at a pace you choose and at your direction.

Client and Caregiver Education

Knowledge is power. We will endeavor to educate you and those who assist you through ongoing communications, newsletters, seminars and publications. If we advise you on a certain course of action, we will endeavor to explain the reasoning behind decision. To the extent you wish to learn more, we will make resources available to you so that you are better informed of the issues facing seniors and the options for address those issues.

Innovative, Custom Solutions

Elder law is a complicated area of law with conflicting rules, regulations and solutions. There are no “cookie cutter” senior care plans. In fact, what worked for one client situation, will probably not work the same effect for another client. Estate planning documents that may have been appropriate a few years ago, may now have an undesirable effect. Why? Because your circumstances have changed, your needs and objectives have changed, or the law has changed. We will work with you to understand your needs, circumstances, resources, and goals. We will then develop a custom estate plan or senior care plan that accomplishes your goals and addresses your needs. As circumstances change, as the law changes, as your needs change, we will work with you to update and modify your plan to ensure it continues to accomplish what you want it to do.

Integrated and Coordinated Services

A thoughtful and effective estate plan or Senior Care Plan most often includes legal services, financial services, care management and medical services. Unfortunately, each of these different types of services providers know little of what the other does. We will endeavor to develop a coordinated plan that addresses each of these areas in a thoughtful and circumspect manner. We pledge to you that we will not interfere with your existing trusted professionals and will not replace them with those of our choosing. If you need the aid of a certain type of professional service provider for which you do not have a professional relationship, we may be able help you find a suitable professional provider.

Value Based Fee Structure

Attorneys generally bill for services on an hourly rate, a percentage or contingency fee basis, or on a flat fee basis. We prefer to utilize a flat fee structure whenever we can as we believe this is in your best interest. Our fee will be based upon the work being performed, the level of expertise required to effectively implement the plan, and the results achieved for you. At your initial consultation, you will know what the total cost will be and you can decide whether or not it makes sense for you. We will rarely be the cheapest attorney but we also rarely be the most expensive. Seniors need to get their plans right the first time, as mistakes may be very costly and not easily correctable. To be honest, you need us to be successful so that we will be around in the future and can continue to work for you and your family. True value doesn’t come in the least expensive plan; it comes in a plan that will work at a fair price. We will demonstrate to you how we can provide value to you in an estate plan or Senior Care Plan we recommend to you. You then can decide whether or not your plan will be valuable enough to you to move forward.

Competent, Knowledgeable, Counsel

We are an elder law firm. We don’t handle criminal, divorce, employment, DUI or any other types of law. This allows us to focus our attention and development on the law that seniors need most. The old adage, “jack of all trades, master of none” is especially applicable to the legal profession. We understand and know how to help seniors—that’s what we do. We pledge to stay current in the developments in the law though ongoing professional education, and membership in professional associations that enhance the elder law community. This will enable us to provide you with the most current and effective plans and strategies available to address your particular needs.

Our Commitments to You
  1. We will treat you with respect and dignity.
  2. We will give prompt attention to your calls and inquires.
  3. We will keep you informed of developments in the law or your matter.
  4. We will endeavor to help you achieve your objectives for your care needs.
  5. We will not “up sale” or recommend to you more than you need.
  6. The plans we recommend to you will work to meet your objectives.
  7. Our fees will be fair and reasonable and provide to you good value.
